Summary

The opportunity

  • Thai people are ready for Jesus
  • We regularly see people drawn to Jesus when they have opportunity to understand
  • We moved here in 2004 with the expectation that God had something big planned. Like prophetic foreshortening we expected quicker timing, but ultimately we see no reason to believe differently.

Thai church history (a few key points)

  • Began 200 years ago.
  • Proselytization illegal until 1900, so nineteenth century missionaries focused on non-citizens: ethnic Chinese and hill tribe groups. But during the 1900's little happened to reach southern, central and northeast Thais.
  • Campus Crusade reported 1 million professions of faith in response to the Jesus Movie campaign in the 1980's. A friend of mine was on one of the teams.
  • In 2004, the Arthur S. DeMoss Foundation distributed over two million copies of their Power for Living booklet in response to TV ads.
  • Reaching Thailand in this Generation: The Thai Church’s National Plan By Dr. Randy Wollf, April 2010
    2010_0512_Reaching_Thailand_in_this_Generation.pdf (worldevangelicals.org)
  • Churches are multiplying: estimated Christian population has at least doubled in our 15 years here, from 0.5% to 1%--a growth rate of about 5% per year.

Experience in Thailand

Expectation on moving here

  • Rapid multiplication--Reach, Teach, Send. Women would share Jesus with their neighborhoods/villages
  • Reach Isaan people--reach Thailand
  • Working backbone--scattered throughout
  • Cultural influence (the "cool" culture)

Reality

  • Apparently genuine professions of faith not leading to life change
  • "Thais receive [Jesus] easily, but have a hard time growing." –Thai pastor
    • คนไทยรับเชื่อง่ายโต้ยาก
  • Parable of the sower--birds, thorns and rocks are everywhere
  • People generally unable to go it alone
  • Churches had difficulty with brokenness
    • People disinvited
    • Prison ministry same--had to start their own church

Important factors to understand in Thai culture

People groups

  • There are some major distinctions.

Isaan/Northern

  • Similar systems
  • Tropical village culture appropriate to its environment
  • Simple
  • Maternal
  • Thai second language (changing)
  • Animist
  • Now--separated families
  • "Find" money
  • Spend, don't save
  • Major languages still mostly unwritten

Tropical village culture

  • Go with the flow
  • Relationship outweighs truth
  • Individualism is discouraged
  • Respect, do not challenge
  • Follow, do not lead
  • Present-focused### Chinese Thai
  • "Earn" money
  • Save, then spend

Southern

  • More aggressive than Isaan
  • But still a lot of the same brokenness

Hill Tribe

  • Religious groups
  • Buddhist-Animist

Muslim

Education paradigm

Some key characteristics

  • Conformity
  • Learn traditional cultural forms
  • Little to no emphasis on abstract reasoning, problem solving etc.
  • No show-and-tell, book reading/reporting, essays, speeches
  • Considerable time spent in group activities
  • Group projects
  • Intramural days
  • Special days, re. Mother's Day, Father's Day, Teacher Day
  • Ram dance (girls)
  • No failing
  • Only fail for non-participation
    • Make-up test
    • Bribes (link to Tanya video)
  • Result
    • Limited core knowledge
  • Limited abstract and analytical thinking ability

Strong moral teaching, but not followed

Religious tolerance

  • "All religions teach you how to be good."
  • Active proselytization frowned upon

Considerateness, not human rights

  • Individualism is tolerated but not celebrated
  • Example: transgender
  • Example: organized labor

Sexuality

  • Historically polygynous (multiple wives)
  • Wives as status
  • Poor: rent
  • Prostitution outlawed 1960
  • Commercialized sex industry started in 1966
  • https://www.bangkokpost.com/thailand/special-reports/1330319/men-and-their-mistresses
  • Apologies for the suggestive photo
  • Written by a non-Thai: Thai cultural critiques are rare
  • Now - active teen culture

New Testament Model

  • Acts 2: spontaneous, expectant, houses and central hangout
  • Ekklesia used loosely. Greek towns had a governing ekklesia, open to all adult males.
  • Clear emphasis on life together far more than meetings
  • No specification of church forms, only hints
  • Suggest form should follow function

Review of Western church model (as adopted by Thais)

Structure

  • Formal Organization
    • Two key organizational styles
      - Congregational, democratic
      - Hierarchical, authoritarian
  • The centrality of the meeting
    • Expectation as main place where God works
    • Not Eph. 4
    • Relatively few use gifts
    • Lots of gifts and resources involved in preparing and doing.
  • Equipping requires far more than hearing (hearing is the beginning)
  • Have seen churches regularly appeal for help with the Sunday meeting, while great work people were doing on elsewhere was rarely mentioned.
  • Experiential
    • Decor, lighting, sound, music, energy
  • Centrality of the sermon

Teaching and practice

  • Individualistic
  • Systematic theology
    • Emphasis on correct belief but often not overtly connected with life
    • Creeds
    • Ordinances
    • Salvation as legal status—actually can become impersonal
  • Obstacles to life together
    • Urban life disconnects people from physical neighbors
    • Net: Now people sit together and chat with other people on their phones.

Dream Church

  • A place for everyone
  • Everyone is a greeter
  • Everyone is a teacher-in-training

Obstacles for discipleship and church development

Lifestyle

  • Immorality
  • Sexuality
  • Lying
  • Value of non-interference
    • Related to tolerance
  • Addictions
    • Substance
    • Sex
    • Gambling
  • Social coping behaviors
    • Co-enabling
    • Expectation of hypocrisy
    • Cover for each other
    • Greatest sin--ratting
  • Extension of Buddhism
    • Tom boon system
    • วันศีล
    • โบสถ์ - อุโบสถ ceremonial building in temple

Religious opposition ( 95% Buddhist)

Economics

  • Growth since 1997
  • People focused on upward mobility
    • Comforts
    • Children's education
  • 6-day work week
    • And many bottom-tier jobs work around labor laws, require 7 days
    • Not the same as traditional 6-day rural life. This is usually work away from home, leaving minimal time for housework or errands.
  • Result: people are busy and tired (all classes)
  • Many people work retail on Sundays
    • Including teens, young adults--an ideal population for outreach
  • Pastors normally will live comfortably and put kids in good schools
    • Expected with higher education
    • Income standard generally higher than working class Thais; makes a challenge for the paid pastor model.
  • Many have extra family burdens
    • Related to sexuality:
      • Teen pregnancies result in economic struggle
      • Blended families--a lot of "his", "hers" and "their" kids
    • Parents, grandparents, younger siblings, incarcerated etc.
    • Addictions
    • Debt
      • Usury
      • Vehicle payments

Education

  • Not challenged to evaluate truth
  • Not reading books/history/current events for meaning and lessons
  • Many drop out before 9th grade, remain stuck at a 4th-6th grade level.
  • The problem with the sermon
  • The ineffectiveness of passive listening is well-known.
  • People with minimal core knowledge have poor ability to add knowledge.
  • Learning is a process of attaching new information to old
  • They may show understanding of a concept but will have difficulty remembering it.
  • Concrete thinkers will have trouble hearing a principle and applying it to their own lives.
  • Thai people need to be shown, not told.

Language

  • Tends to be concrete, lacking key abstract concepts common to Western languages
  • Integrity
  • Denial
  • Belonging
  • Inference
  • Strong distinction between written and spoken language
  • King language

Thais have great difficulty sharing their faith

  • Value of tolerance/non-interference
  • Present-focus
  • More importantly Thais seldom think or talk about world views.
  • Rick Kuter interview with monk
  • Aj. Soontorn quote: "I build relationship, I win their trust. Then I can teach them anything."
    • They are essentially tongue-tied
  • Tend to be very shy, even bashful. Don't talk to strangers out of shyness, not lack of interest.

Class distinctions

  • Universal issue, not just Asian
  • Generally now more latent than overt compared with the past, but still strong

Faith/prayer

  • Weak sense of spiritual reality

Urban life (Bangkok)

  • Loss of community
  • People spread out
  • Little to do

Methodology

Paradigm changes

Reach

  • Visibility
  • Social media
  • Tools
  • Thai "Four-laws equivalent"
  • A presentation
  • Reach people where they are
  • Muslim
  • If you want to go to the owner's house, you have to know the owner.
  • Turn cultural gentleness into a strength, not a liability

Teach

Accessible gatherings

  • Local
  • People are busy and tired.
  • Traveling takes too long
  • Churches typically manage by making Sunday long, but that leaves out people working on Sundays.
  • Frequent/flexible
  • Sunday morning doesn't work at all for:
    • Retail and hotel workers
      • Many are teens/young adults--a key target population
    • Women who work "at night"
  • It can barely work for Monday-Saturday workers (factory and small business), who reserve Sundays for housework, errands and recreation
  • Special need/interest groups (like any church)
  • Couples' banquet
  • Addictions support (rewrite Celebrate Recovery for Thai culture)

Simple teaching

  • Levels of ability/understanding
  • Grade school
    • Reads the words, does not understand many words, minimally understands meaning.
    • Will quickly forget
  • Teaching must be concrete, rote
    - Memorized verses
    - Bible stories and parables
    - Repeated catch phrases
    • Each step requires feedback
  • Personally Interactive
    • Follow Jesus' model--apply principle to context, not state principle and require people to apply to their own context.
    • Individual, whole life.
      "That’s fine but not what this means."
    • Not only Q&A inductive Bible study
      • Involves reading a passage and guiding people in understanding principles
      • Only highly educated and/or exceptionally bright people can do it.
      • E.g. SOAP method--answers to steps 2 and 3 will be all over the place, often completely unrelated to the intent of the passage.
      • Inductive Bible reading must be the goal, but we have to help people get there.
    • Walk with people
  • Bible storytelling
    • What the story tells us about God
    • What the story tells us about ourselves
  • Repeated verbatim principles
    • Gospel (4 spiritual laws or equivalent)
  • Commands of Jesus
  • Verse memorization
  • etc.
