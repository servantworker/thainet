Introduction

Encouragements

  • Many more ministries than 20 years ago
  • Churches have multiplied; Christians doubled from 0.5 to 1%
  • More people doing ministry who have been healed from broken backgrounds

Challenges

  • Sex industry keeps growing, perhaps even more rapidly since COVID. We're not keeping up.
  • Increasing use of social media/web sites of women selling themselves
  • Brokenness keeps growing
  • Drugs are now everywhere. Yaba is so cheap.
  • More and more broken people having children.
  • Possibility that prostitution will be legalized.
  • The needs and risk factors of exploited or at-risk persons are complex

What is Needed

  • Repent of our own self-centeredness, apathy etc.
  • Pray for a new move of God
  • Cooperate for a comprehensive approach
    • Share resources
    • Share know-how
    • Refer cases
    • Speak God's truth to His people and to Thai society
    • Initiate joint projects

Mission

  • As people burdened for the lost and broken, come together to seek, proclaim and demonstrate the Kingdom of God to the people of Thailand through sacrificially and patiently loving people who are vulnerable and hurt, and equipping others to do the same.

Vision

  • A new atmosphere of unity and cooperation by people working in healing and prevention ministry
  • A increasing number of projects that help broken people
  • An increasing number of people growing in vision, maturity and expertise
  • A growing number of churches with increasing openness and ability to reach out, welcome, and care for broken people

Structure

  • Informal, not an organization
  • Based around statement of of faith and values (TBA)
  • Network to be maintained by people called by God to facilitate communication

Social media groups

  • Self-managed social networks, not centralized
  • Groups will form around various scopes and tasks and communicate using a social media platform of their choice (e.g. Line or FB Messenger)
  • Groups to set own rules for membership, confidentiality etc.
  • Directories of groups to be maintained in one or more master locations.
    • Servantworks/Jim Larson to begin but will encourage others to share

Events

  • Gather "house-to-house" for prayer, consultation, training

Example groups

  • General advocacy
  • Education/Training
  • Direct service cooperation
    • Regional
    • Need-based, e.g. mental health

Types of Members

  • Experts: provide consultation and training
  • Partners: directly serve hurting people whether professionally or volunteer
  • Supporters: support others in prayer, finances, encouragement
  • Advocates: people who help communicate needs and issues to others

Expats and Thais in partnership

  • Must work in full partnership but with complementary roles
  • The goal is full Thai ownership and leadership
  • Expats encourage and assist helping Thais to hear from God, beginning with the commands of Jesus, and find the best ways to apply
  • Must work with daily awareness of the strengths and limitations that come from culture, personal giftedness, personality and sinfulness.
  • Must maintain a culture of humble, cooperative learning and improvement.

Expats

  • Positives that westerners bring
    • background of the long history of biblical values
    • exposure to a wide variety of thought and practice
    • access to expertise in areas of mental health, addiction, community development and more
    • multicultural perspective
  • Challenges to westerners
    • Guilt culture--quick fix
    • Tendency to impose Western values, especially independence and individualism
  • Expat Roles
    • Catalysis
    • Resource development
    • Careful coaching
    • Not management

Thais

  • Positives that Thais bring
    • servant hearts deference to others (greng jai)
    • family focus
    • high value of gentleness, carefulness in relationships
  • Challenges to Thais
    • objective, systematic problem solving
    • shame culture--tendency to blame
  • Thai Roles
    • Co-learning
    • Management
    • Resource development

Faith and Value Statement Components

Values

  • Non-territorial
  • Love each other
  • Safe communication
  • Culturally careful
  • Learn from each other
  • Give to each other

Key words

  • Evangelism 
  • Transformation 
  • Unity
  • Proclamation: speak the truth of the Gospel in all matters
  • Demonstration: show Thai people the Kingdom of Heaven through our obedience.
  • Incarnation: live among, fellowship with all. No us-them thinking.
  • Long-term: work patiently leaving results to God's timing; allow people to grow and heal on His schedule.
  • Catalyzing: stimulating vision in others, helping others to also begin ministry.
  • Learning: identify as seekers of wisdom, cf. Proverbs 1-5, always improving
  • Prophetic: speaking God's truth to God's people and to the world
  • Set apart (sacred, holy)

Key Scripture Texts

  • Philippians 2:1-11; 4:1-8
  • Galatians 6:2
  • Isaiah 1:17
  • Psalm 133
  • 1 John 1:7
